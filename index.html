Planting the Seeds of Life.

Planting time varies depending on the region. In temperate climates, rice is typically planted in the spring. Farmers can either sow seeds directly into the flooded paddies or use a nursery to grow seedlings that are later transplanted. Modern farms may use airplanes or specialized drills to ensure precise seed placement.

Beyond the Harvest

Rice farming is a complex and time-honored tradition. It requires knowledge, skill, and a deep respect for the delicate balance between water, soil, and plant life. The next time you enjoy a steaming bowl of rice, take a moment to appreciate the remarkable journey this grain has taken from a humble seed in a flooded field to your table.

Denorado Rice

Dinorado rice is known for its premium quality and is a favorite among Filipinos. Its popularity extends beyond homes, with restaurants, airlines, and hotels often choosing Dinorado for its exceptional taste and texture.

Brown Rice

Brown rice is essentially unprocessed rice. Unlike white rice that has the hull, bran, and germ removed, brown rice only loses the inedible outer hull. This keeps the nutrient-rich bran and germ intact, resulting in the brown color and characteristic texture.

$40

NFA Rice

The NFA (National Food Authority) aims to maintain stable rice prices for consumers, especially low-income families. It maintains a reserve of rice to address emergencies and calamities. This ensures a steady supply of rice even during disruptions like typhoons.
